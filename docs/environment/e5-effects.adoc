[[E5]]
=== E.5 Effects

NOTE: Elements and properties of the environment that are affected by the operation
of the system (see {Handbook}).

The operation of the Smart Home System (SHS) produces observable effects on its
environment. These effects impact physical elements of the dwelling, environmental
conditions, and external actors, without describing the internal implementation
of the system itself.


//---- Effect
[[e5-furniture-reconfiguration]]
^`E.5.{counter:e5}`^  
**Furniture Reconfiguration** :: The physical configuration of `Adaptable furniture`,
including the `Adaptable bed`, may be modified as a result of system operation to
better match the occupantâ€™s needs.
//----


//---- Effect
[[e5-perimeter-state]]
^`E.5.{counter:e5}`^  
**Perimeter State Modification** :: The state of physical access points (doors,
windows) in the MI may change (locked or unlocked), affecting the accessibility of
the dwelling for occupants, intruders, or `Emergency Services`.
//----


//---- Effect
[[e5-environmental-conditions]]
^`E.5.{counter:e5}`^  
**Environmental Conditions Adaptation** :: Lighting levels, temperature, and visual
or luminous signals within the MI environment may be altered, affecting occupant
comfort and safety perception.
//----


//---- Effect
[[e5-external-communication]]
^`E.5.{counter:e5}`^  
**External Communication** :: The system operation may result in the transmission of
alerts, alarms, or contextual information to external actors such as `Emergency
Services` or authorized caregivers.
//----


//---- Effect
[[e5-information-persistence]]
^`E.5.{counter:e5}`^  
**Information Persistence** :: System operation leads to the creation or update of
logs and records, affecting the informational state of the environment and enabling
traceability and auditability.
//----
